# Brand System — One 2b
**Status:** Framework locked — hex codes and logo files pending brand kit drop
**Last updated:** 2026-06-06
**Scope:** All One 2b visual outputs — Studio Social posts, Studio Design assets, Studio Motion, decks, website, email templates.

---

## Identity position

One 2b is the rails, not the interface. The visual language carries that: structural, precise, institutional weight without coldness. Not a fintech startup. Not a bank. The infrastructure layer that makes both possible.

---

## Colour palette

**Primary:**
- Navy (deep) — `[hex pending brand kit]` — backgrounds, primary text, authority anchors
- Gold / warm amber — `[hex pending brand kit]` — accent, highlights, forward-motion elements
- White — pure — breathing room, space, precision

**Secondary (use sparingly):**
- Slate grey — `[hex pending brand kit]` — supporting text, borders
- Warm off-white — `[hex pending brand kit]` — alternative backgrounds

**Never use:** RGB values that produce garish or consumer-brand aesthetics. No pastels. No bright primary colours. The palette is conservative-warm, not corporate-cold.

---

## Typography

**Headlines:** Clean, geometric sans-serif. High weight. Not condensed. Not decorative.
- Preferred: `[font name pending brand kit]`
- Fallback: Inter Bold or equivalent system font

**Body:** Same family, regular weight. Generous line height. Never compressed.

**Never use:** Script fonts, display fonts from Adobe's freemium library, anything that reads as "AI-generated presentation."

---

## Visual language rules

**Imagery style:** Real, earned, specific. Not stock photography. Not generic "business people at laptop." If humans appear, they are in real contexts — working, thinking, in the physical world. The same energy as Jason's personal brand: grounded, not performed.

**Data visualisation:** Clean and minimal. One message per chart. Navy/gold palette. No chart junk.

**Text on image:** White or gold only. Never other colours. Never more than 8 words. Headlines, not paragraphs.

**Layouts:** Structured. Grid-based. Breathing room. No crowding.

---

## Terminology visual rules

Any visual that includes text must comply with Sentinel A1:
- "data-linked insurance products" not "IGI Insurance"
- "Tesseract" (capital T, never Tessaract)
- Asterisk on all yield/return figures
- No named insurance counterparties
- Sovereign reference: Portugal only (live in production)

## Voice-visual consistency

The visual system should feel like the written voice: precise, institutional, warm without being soft. A deck that looks like a startup pitch or a consumer app is out of register. A deck that looks like it belongs in a Lloyd's boardroom is on-register.
