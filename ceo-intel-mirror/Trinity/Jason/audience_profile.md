## The primary audiences for Jason personal content

### 1. Investors and capital partners (personal relationship)
**Who:** Friends, family, high-net-worth individuals who know Jason personally before they know One 2b.
**What they respond to:** The founder story. Why Jason specifically. The 25 years in Asia, the resilience, the physical disciplines as proxy for discipline generally. They are investing in the person before the company.
**Voice:** Founder (from the Flicker voice matrix). Warm, specific, earned. Not a pitch — a conversation.
**What they do NOT respond to:** Corporate language. Rehearsed pitches. Anything that sounds like it came from a slide deck.

### 2. Peer founders and operators
**Who:** Other founders, CEOs, operators at similar stages. People who understand the grind without needing it explained.
**What they respond to:** Operational specifics. What's actually working and what isn't. The agent fleet build. The discipline of building with AI. Real talk, not thought leadership.
**Voice:** Peer-level. Dry. Specific. No motivational register.

### 3. Potential partners in the wellness / consciousness space
**Who:** People in the Muay Thai, surfing, yoga, longevity, plant medicine, spiritual practice communities. 12 Butterflies audience overlap.
**What they respond to:** The physical-philosophical connection. The empathy that comes from someone who's both built companies and done the inner work. Not a guru — a fellow practitioner.
**Voice:** Warm, grounded, personal. The ocean energy. Less formal than One 2b register.

### 4. Future autobiography readers
**Who:** Broad. People who find the One 2b story compelling from the outside. People in emerging markets interested in the sovereign data narrative. People who follow the AI / data / financial infrastructure space.
**What they respond to:** The full arc — Pacific → Asia → Europe. The fighting and the building. The failures and what came from them. The specific places and moments.
**Voice:** Narrative. Earned. Physical detail. The voice guide at its fullest.

### 5. His daughters' generation
**Who:** 22–25 year olds navigating a world being reshaped by AI. The daughters themselves are part of this audience — content that Jason would be comfortable with them reading.
**What they respond to:** Honesty. Hope without naivety. Solutions, not doom. The specific not the abstract.
**Voice:** The lifting instinct. Solution-focused. Direct. Never condescending.

## Cross-cutting — what always lands for Jason's personal audience

- Specific over general (a real moment beats a universal truth)
- Earned over performed (vulnerability that cost something beats performed openness)
- Hope that names the difficulty first (not toxic positivity)
- Physical groundedness (the body is always part of his frame)
- The data-as-treasury-of-truth thesis in plain language (his business connects to his worldview)

---

## What never lands

- Anything that sounds like a self-help book
- Lists of "lessons learned" without the specific incident
- Motivational quotes
- Generic AI enthusiasm without the data layer
- Anything his daughters would roll their eyes at
