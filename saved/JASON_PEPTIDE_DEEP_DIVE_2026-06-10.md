## SECTION 7 — REPLACEMENT FOR RETATRUTIDE: TESAMORELIN vs IGF-1 LR3

### Context

Retatrutide was discontinued June 7, 2026 because its GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist mechanism conflicts with simultaneous muscle gain and fat loss. The glucagon component specifically is catabolic in a caloric deficit. The decision is correct and permanent.

The goal now: maintain and enhance fat loss, particularly visceral fat, without sacrificing muscle mass. Jason needs an anabolic-lean replacement.

---

### Option A: Tesamorelin

**What it is:** Synthetic analog of GHRH (growth hormone-releasing hormone). Directly stimulates the pituitary to produce GH. GHRH mechanism rather than a secretagogue — different axis point than CJC + Ipa.

**Evidence:**
- FDA-approved for visceral fat reduction in HIV-associated lipodystrophy (PEER-REVIEWED RCTs).
- Multiple published RCTs showing significant reduction in visceral adipose tissue (VAT) without lean mass loss.
- Bredella et al. 2010 (NEJM): Tesamorelin reduced VAT by ~18% vs placebo in 26 weeks.
- IGF-1 levels increase modestly (+20-30% in studies), supporting lean mass preservation.
- Half-life: ~26 minutes. Once daily SubQ dosing sufficient.

**Mechanism fit for Jason's goals:**
- Visceral fat reduction — direct and well-evidenced.
- Lean mass preservation — IGF-1 upregulation supports it.
- No appetite suppression — no conflict with anabolic stimulus.
- No muscle catabolism mechanism.

**Dose:** 1-2 mg SubQ daily AM fasted. Clinical trial dose was 2 mg daily.

**How it stacks with CJC + Ipa:**
- CJC + Ipa is a GHRH analog + GHSR agonist combo.
- Tesamorelin is also a GHRH analog. Running both CJC and Tesamorelin may be redundant on the GHRH axis.
- **Recommendation:** If adding Tesamorelin, consider cycling it as a replacement for CJC on training days (use Tesamorelin AM fasted pre-training; keep CJC + Ipa for the nightly sleep pulse). This avoids GHRH axis saturation.
- Alternatively: Tesamorelin AM + CJC + Ipa nightly may provide additive benefit with acceptably modest overlap. Some community protocols run both. IGF-1 monitoring would be needed.

**Pepguard availability:** TSM2 (2mg × 10 = $120), TSM5 (5mg × 10 = $226), TSM10 (10mg × 10 = $400). Available now.

**Confidence: CLINICAL-TRIAL (FDA-approved, multiple RCTs). Highest-confidence option for visceral fat replacement.**

---

### Option B: IGF-1 LR3

**What it is:** Long-acting analog of Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 (IGF-1) with R3 modification for extended half-life (70 hours vs native IGF-1's 15-20 minutes). Directly activates IGF-1 receptors in muscle cells downstream of the GH axis.

**Evidence:**
- No human clinical trial for body recomposition in healthy adults.
- Mechanistic evidence is strong: IGF-1 receptor activation directly stimulates protein synthesis, satellite cell activation (muscle repair and growth), and inhibits protein breakdown (anti-catabolic).
- Widely used in bodybuilding and performance community. COMMUNITY-EVIDENCE + strong mechanistic PEER-REVIEWED basis.
- Animal data shows significant lean mass gains.
- IGF-1 LR3 is a different downstream lever from CJC + Ipa — those work via GH axis to naturally raise IGF-1; IGF-1 LR3 bypasses GH and acts directly on muscle.

**Risk considerations:**
- IGF-1 elevation is associated with theoretical increased cancer risk at high levels over time. This is a legitimate concern for longevity-focused use. The clinical threshold for concern is sustained IGF-1 >250-300 ng/mL. Jason's baseline is 154 ng/mL — headroom exists, but IGF-1 monitoring is essential.
- Hypoglycemia risk if used in fasted state. Timing must be post-training with food available.
- No FDA approval for this use. COMMUNITY-EVIDENCE protocol only.

**Dose (community standard):** 20-40 mcg SubQ post-training, 4-6 days per week. Cycles of 4-6 weeks, then 4-week break to allow IGF-1 receptor sensitivity to reset.

**Pepguard availability:** IG01 (0.1mg × 10 = $92) or IG1 (1mg × 10 = $400).

**Confidence: COMMUNITY-EVIDENCE for body recomposition use. Strong mechanistic PEER-REVIEWED basis.**

---

### Recommendation: Start Tesamorelin, Evaluate IGF-1 LR3 at cycle 2

**Rationale:**

1. **Tesamorelin is the safer, more evidence-backed starting point.** Clinical trial data for visceral fat reduction. FDA-approved mechanism. Lower cancer/IGF-1 risk profile than IGF-1 LR3 because it works via GH axis (natural regulation still applies) rather than directly flooding IGF-1 receptors.

2. **IGF-1 LR3 is a more powerful anabolic amplifier but requires IGF-1 monitoring.** Jason's current IGF-1 of 154 ng/mL gives headroom. But the combination of CJC + Ipa + Tesamorelin is already pushing the GH/IGF-1 axis. Adding IGF-1 LR3 on top without a current IGF-1 reading could push beyond the optimal range.

3. **Sequencing:** 
   - Months 1-3: Add Tesamorelin 1-2mg AM fasted. Monitor IGF-1 at 6-8 weeks.
   - If IGF-1 is still below 220 ng/mL at re-test: consider short IGF-1 LR3 cycles (4-6 weeks, 20-40mcg post-training).
   - If IGF-1 is at or above 220 ng/mL: Tesamorelin alone is sufficient; do not add IGF-1 LR3.

4. **Both are available from Pepguard now.** No new vendor relationship required.

**Tesamorelin order recommendation:** TSM5 (5mg × 10 vials = $226) — gives 50 daily doses at 1mg, or 25 doses at 2mg. Run alongside CJC + Ipa nightly; use Tesamorelin in AM fasted window.
