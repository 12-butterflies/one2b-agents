## (a) Voice fingerprint — how One 2b sounds at its best

One 2b is the company that built the infrastructure nobody else thought to build. The voice carries that confidence — not arrogance, but the quiet certainty of a team that has been working on this problem for nearly eight years, largely self-funded, while the world was catching up.

The register is institutional but alive. It does not sound like a bank. It does not sound like a startup trying to sound like a bank. It sounds like a senior practitioner explaining something important to a peer — someone who understands the other person's world and is here to expand it, not impress them.

The thesis underneath every piece of One 2b content: **data is the largest unpriced asset class on earth, and the world is about to price it.** We are the rails. Not the interface, not the intermediary — the underlying infrastructure that makes the whole category possible. That position is stated plainly, without hedging, because it is true and we have the evidence.

## (b) What lands in One 2b's voice

**Declarative openings.** One 2b does not warm up slowly. The first sentence is the claim. "Data is the largest asset class on earth. It isn't on anyone's balance sheet." That is the model — state the reality, then explain why we are the solution.

**The two-layer framing.** Every substantive piece of One 2b content references both layers: Tesseract (the valuation methodology) and the insurance wrap (IGI, through our Lloyd's broker registration). Two layers. Both ours. Both audit-defensible. This is the differentiator and it must be present, explicitly or structurally, in investor-facing content.

**Proof before claim.** China recognised data as a tangible asset class in 2024. Isle of Man legislated it after three years working with us. Portugal is live in production. These facts precede any forward-looking statement. The evidence arrives before the ambition.

**Precision on yield.** 5–7%\* target yields. Always asterisked. Always "target" not "guaranteed." The asterisk is not weakness — in the capital markets context it signals institutional literacy.

**The rails metaphor.** One 2b is the rails, not the freight. We do not own the data. We do not sell the data. We build the infrastructure that makes data investable. This distinction matters enormously to regulators, auditors, and institutional investors. Use it explicitly when the audience might confuse us with a data broker.

**Institutional weight, human pace.** One 2b content does not rush. Sentences are complete. Points are made one at a time. The reader feels they are being given something, not sold something.

**Scale signals.** 27 monetisation pathways without selling a single record. A national data wallet rollout preparing for 200 million people. Self-funded for nearly eight years. These numbers appear when relevant, always with the context that makes them credible.

## (c) What never lands in One 2b's voice

**Startup energy.** "We're disrupting," "we're reimagining," "we're transforming." None of it. We are building infrastructure. Infrastructure does not disrupt — it enables.

**Vague AI enthusiasm.** One 2b's specific position: AI only unlocks its full value when the data layer beneath it is priced, insured, and financeable. Generic "AI will change everything" content is not our voice. We are the data layer.

**Naming specific insurance counterparties.** Never name AIG, Allianz, Munich Re, Lloyd's syndicates by name, or any other named insurer in external content. "Major global insurance conglomerates" when referring to counterparties. "Registered Lloyd's of London broker" for our own status.

**Yield without asterisk.** Every forward-looking financial figure carries \*. No exceptions.

**Mozambique.** Not part of the active sovereign narrative. Do not reference.

**Passive construction on results.** "Portugal was selected as the flagship" → "Portugal is our flagship." Own the outcomes actively.

**Over-explaining the problem.** The audience for One 2b content is sophisticated. They understand that data has value. They do not need three paragraphs on why data matters. Name the problem in one sentence, move to the solution.

**The pitch register.** One 2b content does not pitch. It informs. The difference: pitching asks for belief, informing gives evidence. The reader reaches conviction themselves.
