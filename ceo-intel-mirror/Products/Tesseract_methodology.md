## The three approaches (all applied and reconciled)

**Cost Approach**
What would it cost to recreate this dataset from scratch?
Inputs: data collection cost, processing cost, human curation cost, time to replicate, IP development cost.
Best for: Unique or proprietary datasets where no market comparable exists.

**Market Approach**
What do comparable data assets trade for?
Inputs: named comparable transactions (with source and date — no fabricated comps), market liquidity signals, premium/discount factors.
Best for: When transaction data exists in the domain (financial data, geospatial data, consumer behaviour data).

**Income Approach**
What income can this data generate over its useful life?
Inputs: current revenue from data licensing (if any), projected revenue from commercialisation pathways, discount rate, useful life estimate.
Best for: Data assets with proven or projectable revenue streams.

All three approaches are documented in the valuation report. The final figure is a synthesised value with documented weighting rationale — the weighting itself is subject to Sentinel A3 review before the report is delivered.

## Data quality scoring (IVSC criteria)

| Criterion | What we assess |
|---|---|
| Completeness | What % of expected data fields are populated |
| Accuracy | Error rate, validation against known sources |
| Consistency | Cross-dataset agreement, format standardisation |
| Timeliness | Age of data, refresh frequency |
| Uniqueness | Duplication rate, deduplication methodology |
| Provenance | Chain of custody, consent framework, collection methodology |

Minimum quality score required for valuation: defined per engagement, disclosed in intake report.

## What the methodology does NOT do

- Assign a value to personal data without a clear, audited consent framework
- Produce a valuation for synthetic or AI-generated data presented as real
- Generate market comparables without named sources and dates
- Fabricate precision — if a data point cannot be sourced, it is absent with explanation

## Audit-defensible workpapers

Every Tesseract report includes:
- Methodology section (which approaches applied, why, weighting rationale)
- Data quality scorecard
- Comparable transaction table (with sources, dates, discount/premium factors)
- Sensitivity analysis (valuation range under different assumptions)
- Limitations and material uncertainties section

These workpapers are designed to survive audit firm scrutiny. The methodology is peer-reviewable by Big 4 data valuation practices.
