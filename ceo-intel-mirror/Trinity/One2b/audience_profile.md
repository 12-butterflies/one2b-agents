## The seven audiences and how One 2b speaks to each

### 1. FOR INVESTORS
**Who:** Private investors, family offices, HNWIs considering the current round.
**Their frame:** Portfolio diversification, early-mover positioning in a new asset class.
**Hook:** "AI just became the world's largest industry. The asset it runs on is the largest asset class on earth. And it isn't on anyone's balance sheet."
**Their need:** A defensible number on data, backed by insurance, that fits inside a portfolio.
**Key proof points:** Registered Lloyd's of London broker. Portugal flagship sovereign live in production. Isle of Man legislated after 3 years working with us. China recognised data as asset class 2024. Round is small and timed.
**What they care about:** Preferential pre-money. Window closing. Not missing the category.
**CTA:** 20-minute conversation or investor snapshot.

---

### 2. FOR PEOPLE
**Who:** Individuals aware that their data has value but earning nothing from it.
**Their frame:** Data rights, personal ownership, being early.
**Hook:** "Your data is the most valuable thing you own. You earn nothing from it. Yet."
**Their need:** To understand that the infrastructure for owning and monetising personal data is being built now.
**Key proof points:** National data wallet rollout preparing for 200 million people. Pilot live with a sovereign nation. First countries recognising data rights.
**What they care about:** Being early. Not being left behind. Their data paying them like a savings account.
**Tone:** Accessible, hopeful, not technical.

---

### 3. FOR COMPANIES
**Who:** CEOs, CFOs, business owners sitting on years of accumulated data.
**Their frame:** Balance sheet, leverage, lending, investor confidence.
**Hook:** "Your data is your most valuable asset. Worth zero on your balance sheet. Until now."
**Their need:** A real number their CFO can stand behind, with insurance so their bank can trust it.
**Key proof points:** Accounting rules now allow it. Real number + insurance = leverage. Lease it. Lend against it. Insure it.
**What they care about:** No number = no leverage. Being early on a new asset class is rare.
**Tone:** Commercial, direct, CFO-ready.

---

### 4. FOR ACCOUNTING FIRMS
**Who:** Big 4 and mid-tier audit/advisory firms whose clients are asking about data valuation.
**Their frame:** Methodology partnership, audit-defensible workpapers, first-mover advantage in a new practice area.
**Hook:** "Your clients are asking what their data is worth. The methodology to sign off on a number is here."
**Their need:** Independent, institutional-grade methodology that sits above their assurance work — defensible, audit-ready.
**Key proof points:** Independent methodology. Insurance wrapper. Audit-defensible at every step. Enterprise, sovereign, and investor clients all need it.
**What they care about:** Being the methodology partner from the start. Not being behind when the standard firms.
**Tone:** Professional, peer-to-peer, methodology-first.

---

### 5. FOR CAPITAL MARKETS
**Who:** Institutional allocators, fund managers, asset management teams with long-horizon mandates.
**Their frame:** Uncorrelated yield in a crowded market, macro positioning on AI infrastructure.
**Hook:** "AI capex is the macro trade of the decade. Nobody is pricing the underlying."
**Their need:** 5–7%* yield after insurance costs, non-correlated, institutional-grade, insurance-wrapped.
**Key proof points:** Defensible, insurable, financeable. Registered Lloyd's broker. Direct investment. Secured lending. Insurance products. The accounting framework exists.
**What they care about:** Non-correlation. Yield in a rate-normalising environment. Being early on a category.
**Tone:** Institutional, precise, macro-aware.

---

### 6. FOR DATA LEADERS
**Who:** CDOs, CDAOs, heads of data inside enterprises and governments.
**Their frame:** Turning data from cost centre to balance-sheet asset. Arguing for asset-level treatment internally.
**Hook:** "You own the most valuable thing in the company. And you cannot defensibly prove what it is worth."
**Their need:** Institutional-grade methodology + insurance wrap that makes the CDO's case defensible to the CFO, board, and auditors. Then data leasing rails for the next phase.
**Key proof points:** Audit-grade methodology. Insurance wrapping it. Data leasing: lease, licence, insure, securitise — 90% of enterprise data locked in vaults, unlocked for AI training without selling a record.
**What they care about:** Defining the category from inside. Moving from "data is strategic" to "data is on the books."
**Tone:** Peer-level, technically credible, ambitious.

---

### 7. FOR SOVEREIGNS
**Who:** Ministries of finance, sovereign development banks, central banks, national data offices.
**Their frame:** New fiscal instrument, sovereign balance sheet expansion, national data strategy.
**Hook:** "Your nation holds the largest unrecognised asset on its books. The framework to recognise it is here."
**Their need:** Defensible methodology + insurance that makes sovereign data recognition actionable — without multilateral guarantees, without adding to public debt.
**Key proof points:** Portugal flagship live. Brazil and Colombia this year. Tanzania, Mozambique, Gabon, Argentina advancing. 200-million-person national data wallet rollout. Sovereign-grade architecture.
**What they care about:** Fiscal capacity without debt. First-mover status in a new instrument. Sovereign data rights.
**Tone:** Ministerial, formal, sovereignty-affirming.
