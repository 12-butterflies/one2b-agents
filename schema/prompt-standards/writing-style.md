# Writing Style Rules — One 2b / Jason Peter Stevens
**Locked:** 2026-06-08
**Applies to:** All agents, all surfaces, all formats. No exceptions.

---

## Punctuation

**NO HYPHENS.**
Never use a hyphen in any written output. Not in prose, not in lists, not in subject lines, not in WhatsApp messages, not in decks, not in emails.

**ALWAYS USE A COMMA.**
Wherever a hyphen might be tempting, use a comma instead. If a comma does not fit grammatically, restructure the sentence until it does.

Examples:
- Wrong: "data-linked products"
- Right: "data linked products"

- Wrong: "first-of-its-kind"
- Right: "first of its kind"

- Wrong: "well-established"
- Right: "well established"
