Before we get into the week, I want to take a moment for something that deserves to be said out loud.

Carl has spent three weeks doing work that I think will change the shape of every conversation we have with a CFO from here forward.

Here's what he built.

**The question he set out to answer** was: can we prove — across multiple countries, multiple auditor types, and multiple regulatory frameworks — that data assets can be recognised as Level 3 financial assets on a balance sheet, through an insurance wrapper, without a lender and without an SPV?

The answer is yes. And he has 230 real-world confirmed examples to prove it.

**How the mechanism works, in plain English:**

The company declares legal separability of a specific data asset. The insurer issues a certificate — this becomes the unit of account. The auditor does exactly one thing: confirms the fair value has been calculated correctly under IVSC methodology. That's it. The auditor doesn't originate anything. The company makes the statement. The insurer validates it. The auditor reviews the maths. Result: a non-monetary Level 3 financial asset on the balance sheet under IFRS. No lender. No SPV. Clean.

Carl found the first confirmed instance of this in an Australian agricultural dataset case — closed-loop, lenderless, proprietary sensor mapping data. That was his hook. From there, country by country, sector by sector, he built outward.

**What he built:**

- 15 countries validated: China, UAE, Canada, South Africa, Australia, Liechtenstein, Germany, Sweden, Chile, Colombia, Poland, Malaysia, Singapore, Isle of Man — and more
- 230 confirmed real-world examples — every one documented: data asset type, insurance structure, contract duration, USD-equivalent insured sum
- 100 China examples alone, including BYD (battery stress systems), Lenovo (warehouse metrics), and Tencent-scale companies
- 130 additional examples spanning clean tech, automotive telematics, tokenised fine art, Web3 blockchain staking rewards as Isle of Man Class 12 capital policy
- A spread of Big Four auditors across the full set — not one firm, the full breadth
- Country-specific compliance checklists — China, Germany, Australia each sit differently under the regs, and Carl has a version for each

The sources are Big Four reports and regulatory filings. Private balance sheets aren't accessible, but the captive and insurance wrapper structures were all verifiable. These are confirmed structures — not analogies, not estimates.

**Why this matters for our pipeline right now:**

Until this week, when we sit in front of a CFO and they say "I've never seen this done before" — we've had to fight through that objection every time. That objection is now permanently eliminated.

We can say: BYD did it. Lenovo did it. Companies across 15 countries and every major sector have done it. We are not inventing a category. We are making a proven category accessible.
